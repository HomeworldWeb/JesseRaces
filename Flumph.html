Flumph

Flumphs generally live in small communities called cloisters in the underdark, and often help adventurers by telling them about evil creatures. A flumph may feel out of place among their own kind, and join a party of adventurers on a quest instead of just helping them once.
    Flumphs do not have a biological sex, and they do not generally have names, but they may adopt a name and gender when interacting with other races who have them.

    Ability Score Change. Your Strength decreases by 2, and either Dexterity increases by 3, or you increase two ability scores by one each. Your Dexterity maximum is increased to 21. You can also either increase one ability score by 2 and another by 1, or increase three ability scores by 1.
    Age. Flumphs reached maturity at 3 years old and tipically lived to around 20.
    Alignment. Flumphs are almost all lawful good, each doing their job to support the cloister. This does not mean their cannot be exceptions.
    Size. Flumphs resemble jellyfish with two eyestalks emerging from the top. Your size is Small.
    Speed. Flumphs fly by siphoning air through their bodies, producing the sound that gave them their name, although they find the name "flumph" offensive. Your base walking speed is 5 feet, and you have a base flying speed of 30 feet.
    Type.You are an Aberration.
    Advanced Telepathy. You can perceive the content of any telepathic communication used within 60 you, and you can't be suprised by creatures with any form of telepathy.
    Darkvision. You have superior vision in dark and dim conditions. You can see in dim light within 60 feet of you as if it were bright light, and in darkness as if it were dim light. You can't discern colors in darkness, only shades of gray.
    Different Anatomy. You cannot wear armor unless it was custom made for your anatomy, and any armor you start with is. You also may not be able to attune to some magic items, at the DM's discression.
    Multiple Tendrils. You may weild three weapons that all have the light property and attack with all of them when you multiattack.
    Obvious Emotions. When you try to hide your emotions, in addition to a Charisma(Deception) check, you must make a Constitution check, as flumphs normally glow in accordance to their emotion, and brighter more more intense emotions. The DM decides whether you succeed or not based on the check and the intensity of the emotion you are trying to hide. Red is anger, yellow is fear, and orange is a combination, and might be shone in battle. Pink is for amusement, green for curiosity, and blue for sadness.
    Prone Deficiency. If you are knocked prone, you must roll 1d6. If you roll an odd number, you must make a Strength or Dexterity (your choice) saving throw with a DC equal to 11 + the number rolled. If you fail the save, you are upside-down and thus incapacitated. At the end of each of your turns, you can make a DC 10 Dexterity saving throw, righting yourself and ending the incapacitated condition if you succeed.
    Stench Spray. As an action, you can spray foul smelling liquid in a 15 foot cone originating from yourself. All creatures within that area must succeed on a Dexterity saving throw or be coated in the liquid, the DC of which equals 8 + your proficiency bonus + your Constitution modifier.
    Tendrils. Your unarmed strike counts as a finesse weapon, and it does 1d4 + the ability score modifier you used for the attack modifier piercing damage, plus 1d4 acid damage, as you smear the acid secreted by your tendrils on your target. In addition, at the end of each of it's turns, the target must make a constitution saving throw thats DC is 8 + your proficiency bonus + your constitution modifier and take 1d4 additional acid damage acid damage on a failure or ending the recurring acid damage on a success. A lesser restoration spell cast on the target also ends the recurring acid damage. The recurring damage increases to 2d4 at level 5.
    Languages. You understand undercommon and one other language but cannot speak. You can also use telepathy within a range of 60 feet.
